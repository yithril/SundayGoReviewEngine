🟢 Novice Go Skills

Basic Shape Knowledge
Recognizing strong and weak stone shapes so you don’t accidentally create cutting points or self-atari.

Counting Liberties
Tracking how many breathing spaces a group has to determine whether it lives, dies, or can capture.

Counting Territory
Estimating enclosed points to understand who is ahead on the board.

Defending Cutting Points
Identifying and reinforcing weak connections before your opponent splits your stones apart.

🟡 Beginner Go Skills

Capturing Races
Comparing liberties between opposing groups to determine who wins the race to capture.

Corner Moves in the Opening
Understanding why corners are efficient early and how to approach or defend them.

End Game
Finding the largest remaining point and securing small but crucial territory gains.

Ko Fighting
Managing repeated capture cycles by creating and evaluating ko threats.

Ladders
Reading forcing capture sequences that zigzag across the board in a predictable pattern.

Life and Death
Determining whether a group can make two eyes or can be killed.

Nets
Using surrounding stones to trap a group even if it seems to have liberties.

Shortage of Liberties
Recognizing when a capture fails because the capturing stones run out of liberties first.

🟠 Intermediate Go Skills

Basics of Strategy
Balancing territory and influence while coordinating stones toward a larger plan.

End Game
Evaluating sente vs gote and calculating precise point values in local positions.

Joseki
Playing standard corner sequences that result in balanced outcomes for both players.

Life and Death
Reading deeper variations involving sacrifices, ko, and tesuji inside unsettled groups.

Opening
Building frameworks efficiently while responding properly to approaches and invasions.

Tesuji
Finding sharp tactical moves that create unexpected advantages in local fights.

🔴 Advanced Go Skills

Fighting Technique
Handling complex battles with correct timing, shape, and sacrifice decisions.

Joseki
Understanding not just the sequence, but when and why to deviate based on whole-board context.

Life and Death
Solving difficult positions that require deep reading and non-obvious vital points.

Opening
Choosing direction of play based on thickness, influence, and future fighting potential.

Positional Judgement
Assessing the entire board to decide whether to attack, defend, invade, or reduce.

Pushing Battles / Center Control
Managing running fights and influence in the center without overextending.

🟣 Expert Level

Balance
Maintaining harmony between attack and defense, territory and influence, speed and solidity.

End Game
Calculating exact point values and sequencing moves for maximum efficiency.

Fighting Technique
Executing complex fights with precise reading and timing.

Joseki

Flexibility
Adapting plans fluidly when the board changes unexpectedly.

Life and Death
Reading long, branching variations under pressure with minimal error.

Positional Judgement

Reading
