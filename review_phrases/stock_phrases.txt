Beginner Direction Advice

You can also include very simple advice lines:

“Connecting your stones is usually safer than extending too far.”

“Try to avoid leaving cutting points between your stones.”

“Strong shapes like the bamboo joint are very reliable.”

Example paragraph (30–20 kyu)

Example output:

The opening developed normally, and you used a knight’s move to extend your position. Later you formed a bamboo joint, which is a strong shape that keeps your stones connected. However, one group became weak because the stones were separated. Your opponent placed the group in atari and eventually captured it. Try to focus on keeping your stones connected and avoiding broken shapes.

This is exactly the kind of feedback beginners understand.
